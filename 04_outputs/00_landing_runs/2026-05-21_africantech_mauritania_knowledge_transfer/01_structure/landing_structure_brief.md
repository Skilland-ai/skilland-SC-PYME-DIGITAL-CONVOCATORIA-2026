## 2. Working title

Skilland Knowledge Transfer Engine for European and International Cooperation Projects

## 3. Landing objective

Convert interest from European consortia and international cooperation project teams into a project-fit conversation by showing that Skilland is not just a platform, but a knowledge transfer system that turns project knowledge into reusable, measurable, and scalable training infrastructure.

## 4. Audience

- Primary audience:
  - project coordinators
  - consortium leads
  - work-package owners linked to dissemination, capacity building, exploitation, or training
  - chambers of commerce and public or institutional actors running cooperation programmes
- Secondary audience:
  - project managers
  - dissemination and communication leads
  - capacity-building managers
  - experts or partner entities that need a shared training layer
- Anti-fit / who this is not for:
  - teams looking only for a generic LMS to host existing content
  - buyers expecting a plug-and-play course catalogue unrelated to project knowledge
  - campaigns that position Skilland as standard e-learning software

## 5. Offer framing

- Main offer:
  - Skilland as a Knowledge Transfer System / Knowledge Transfer Engine for European and international cooperation projects.
- Main promise:
  - Skilland helps projects move from dissemination and isolated outputs to real knowledge transfer by converting expert knowledge, project results, and field experience into reusable, measurable, and scalable training systems.
- What the promise means:
  - capture project knowledge
  - structure it into learning assets
  - deliver it through a shared training environment
  - measure participation and usage
- What the promise does NOT mean:
  - guaranteed project impact
  - a claim that Skilland replaces project strategy
  - a generic LMS pitch
  - a claim that every project deployment is already a closed case study

## 6. CTA logic

- Primary CTA:
  - EN: Book a project fit session
  - ES: Agendar sesión de encaje
- Secondary CTA:
  - EN: See how it works
  - ES: Ver cómo funciona
- CTA destination / placeholder:
  - `#contact`

## 7. Narrative hierarchy

1. Reframe the category: this is knowledge transfer, not platform software.
2. Name the real project pain: valuable knowledge gets trapped in reports, events, PDFs, and isolated deliverables.
3. Show the opportunity: project outputs can become living learning infrastructure.
4. Explain the system: capture, structure, deliver, and measure.
5. Defend differentiation: not just another LMS.
6. Show fit with European consortia and international cooperation settings.
7. Show how a shared ecosystem works across partners, experts, and beneficiaries.
8. Translate the model into practical activations and operating steps.
9. Add functional clarity for project managers.
10. Frame AfricanTech as real deployment inspiration without overstating proof.
11. Clear objections.
12. Close with fit-session CTA.

## 12. Claim risks

| Risk | Severity | Why it matters | Safe handling |
|---|---|---|---|
| Positioning drift into generic LMS | High | Breaks the core category claim. | Keep “knowledge transfer system / engine” language throughout. |
| Overstating AfricanTech as proven case study | High | Source explicitly asks for prudence. | Use “built from real European project needs” and “inspired by real deployment needs.” |
| Promising measurable impact as guaranteed outcome | Medium | Measurement is a capability, not a guaranteed result. | Say Skilland helps measure participation, usage, completion, and feedback. |
| Inventing partner logos or ecosystem entities beyond source | Medium | Would add unsupported proof. | Use text pills or only approved copied assets. |
| Overcompressing the argument | High | User explicitly banned simplifying away the core argument. | Preserve the problem -> opportunity -> system -> differentiation chain. |

## 13. What copy must NOT invent

- a different main hero or campaign than “From dissemination to real knowledge transfer”
- a different product category such as generic LMS, academy, or course marketplace
- unsupported performance claims
- unconfirmed booking URL
- extra case study outcomes, deployment metrics, or partner endorsements
- new sections that replace the source narrative
